3   INTRODUCTION In this case study assessment, we will delve into the significance of Social Capital within organizations, particularly in the context of complex, cross-functional IT projects. Social Capital refers to the networks, relationships, and trust that facilitate collaboration and communication among individuals and teams. In today's interconnected work environment, the ability to navigate and leverage these relationships is crucial for project success. Furthermore, we will explore strategies for leveraging Social Capital to secure buy-in from Senior Management, which is often a critical factor in the successful implementation of IT projects. Gaining the support of senior leaders involves not only presenting compelling project proposals but also building strong relationships with key stakeholders. 1.1 OVERVIEW The RSVP team is currently working at MoonTrust Bank in the IT (Information Technology) department. The team holds various designations, such as Developer, QA Tester, Architect, and Project Lead. MoonTrust Bank began its journey in 1920 and currently has 850 employees serving 3.5 million customers in the United States. The bank offers a variety of products, including Checking and savings accounts, Credit Cards, Mortgages, and Small Business and Commercial Lending. The Corporate office of the bank consists of the Legal, IT, Credit, Finance, Marketing, and Operations departments. Each department is headed by an Executive who reports to the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of the bank.  1.2 BACKGROUND The bank adopts a legacy approach to project implementation, wherein projects are evaluated based on their anticipated business impact and the effort required for execution. This methodology prioritizes initiatives according to both business objectives and customer needs, ensuring that resources are allocated to projects deemed most critical for achieving

Gaining the necessary buy-in from multiple cross-functional leaders can be time-consuming and complex, as each leader brings their own perspectives and priorities to the table (Griffin, 2019). Consequently, the approval process can become protracted, delaying important initiatives that could enhance the bank's competitive edge and customer satisfaction. Moreover, the operational dynamics within the bank further complicate project implementation. Each department tends to function in isolation, which creates silos that hinder effective communication and collaboration among teams. This lack of shared intent among both leaders and employees can lead to discrepancies in opinion regarding project value and objectives, resulting in conflicts and misaligned efforts. Addressing these silos and fostering a culture of collaboration could significantly enhance project implementation processes and improve the bank’s ability to respond to the evolving needs of its customers (Griffin, 2019). 1.3 USE OF SOCIAL CAPITAL TO LEVERAGE BUY-IN FROM SENIOR MANAGEMENT In the following sections of the case study assessment, we will discuss the various Organizational Behaviors of MoonTrust Bank, and its employees and how the RSVP team overcame these issues to secure buy-in from Senior Leaders using Social Capital. The RSVP team adopted an approach of creating and mobilizing Social Capital through repeated conversations with Senior Leaders to build trust, leveraging this credibility to gain support from Senior Management in complex IT projects. 1.4 APPROACH The RSVP team will conduct a thorough SWOT analysis to evaluate the organizational behavior challenges at MoonTrust Bank. STRATEGIC ANALYSIS AND IDENTIFYING THE PROBLEM 2.1 PROBLEM IDENTIFICATION The problem MoonTrust Bank is facing difficulty in obtaining top management support for IT projects that are too complex to convert into direct metrics, particularly with Enterprise resource planning implementation. There are lot of challenges in influencing top management, in the past procurement division’s lack of support delayed the project with poor implementation quality. This failure arises because of insufficient social capital – the relationships, trust, and shared understanding. For better project implementation the IT department needs proper resource allocation, top management participation and commitment. As an example, in a healthcare organization, it was observed in a study that employees with strong internal social networks were more successful in influencing senior leaders to adapt newer technologies. This social capital was built through collaboration, shared goals and fostering relationships across various departments (Cross, R., & Prusak, L. (2002).  2.2 STRATEGIC ANALYSIS A strategic analysis of MoonTrust Bank’s ERP project implementation would be having support from all the divisions i.e. finance, procurement, marketing etc because of social capital. SOLUTION/RECOMMENDATIONS 3.1 PROPOSED RECOMMENDATIONS: To resolve MoonTrust Bank’s organizational challenges, we propose the following: 3.1.1 STRENGTHEN CROSS-DEPARTMENTAL COLLABORATION  • Cross-Functional Task Forces: Establish teams with members from IT, finance, marketing, and procurement to improve collaboration and shared understanding of the ERP project. • Regular Meetings and Leadership Workshops: Schedule monthly meetings across departments and workshops for senior leaders to align the ERP goals with the bank’s strategic objectives. 3.1.2 EMPOWER MIDDLE MANAGEMENT • Delegate Decision-Making Authority: Grant middle managers the autonomy to make project-related decisions, enabling them to bridge departmental gaps (Tsai, 1998) and gain support for the ERP project. • Advocacy Role: Equip middle managers with resources to champion the ERP project in their departments. 3.1.3 IMPROVE COMMUNICATION AND TRANSPARENCY • Regular Progress Updates: Implement consistent reporting through dashboards and updates to keep senior management informed of project milestones. • Feedback Channels: Create mechanisms, such as surveys and focus groups, to gather feedback from all departments. 3.1.4 LEVERAGE SOCIAL CAPITAL
