INTRODUCTION 
The Ragland department store, once a staple of its small-town community, is now facing 
Profitability challenges due to competition from large national retailers on the city’s outskirts. To 
revive Performance and support the local downtown revitalization effort, Ragland’s management 
has engaged our consulting team to explore the strategies that can help regain its edge. This 
paper advocates for the implementation of a Balanced Scorecard approach (BSC). The BSc is 
particularly beneficial for mid-sized and small enterprises navigating competitive markets is it 
helps to find underperforming areas early. It supports Ragland’s vision by integrating customer 
centric and community related goals together with financial recovery. 
BALANCED SCORECARD 
A well-structured BSC transforms the store’s mission and strategy into a clear set of 
performance measures across four perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Business 
Processes, and Learning and Growth (Kaplan & Norton, 1992). Below is a proposed Balanced 
Scorecard designed for Ragland Department Store:

PERFORMANCE MEASURES 
Financial Perspective 
1. Gross Margin %:  
Gross Margin Percentage measures the profit we retain after accounting for the cost of 
goods sold. As Ragland battles national chains with their economies of scale, improving this 
metric becomes critical to maintain pricing competitiveness while preserving profitability.  
2. Sales Growth Rate:  
This metric tracks YoY revenue changes. The rate must increase through downtown 
revitalization initiatives and targeted marketing to recapture market share. This metric serves as 
the ultimate indicator of whether our revitalization strategy is gaining traction with customers. 
3. Operating Cost Ratio:  
Calculated as operating expenses divided by total revenue, this ratio reveals how 
efficiently we manage store operations. Reducing this ratio through process automation and 
energy-efficient store upgrades will free up capital to be utilized in other areas.

Page 4
4. Inventory Turnover:  
Turnover measures how quickly we sell through stock. Improving turnover through better 
demand forecasting and lean inventory practices can reduce carrying costs by 15-20%, directly 
boosting bottom-line performance. 
Customer Perspective 
1. CSAT:  
CSAT scores (1-5 scale from post-visit surveys) quantify service quality. Improving the 
score to 4.5+ within 18 months require focused staff training on personalized service - a key 
differentiator for downtown retailers (Verhoef et al., 2019) Each 0.5 point increase correlates with 
7-9% revenue growth in department stores. 
2. Repeat Purchase Rate:  
This measures the percentage of customers returning within 90 days. Implementing a 
modern CRM system and loyalty program can boost this metric by 3-5% annually. Our 
calculations show 1% increase in repeat customers generates $150K in annual revenue. 
3. Downtown Market Share:  
We track our sales as a percentage of total downtown retail sales. We need to work 
towards increasing the share through strategic partnerships with downtown businesses and 
community events as this would position Ragland as the anchor retailer amongst others.  
4. Online Reviews Rating:  
Our average star rating on Google/Yelp influences younger shoppers. With only 3.9 stars 
across platforms, improving to 4.3+ stars require systematic review management and resolution 
of common complaints about checkout waits and stock availability.  
Internal Processes Perspective 
1. Checkout Wait Time:

Page 5
This is the average minutes customers spend in line. We need to work towards reducing 
the current checkout wait times to avoid exceeding the 5-minute threshold that triggers customer 
dissatisfaction (Queuing Theory Research, MIT 2022). Implementing mobile checkout stations 
and optimizing staff scheduling can reduce this, potentially improving CSAT by 0.8 points. 
2. Stock Availability Rate:  
We measure how often top-selling items are in stock (target: 98%). Improving 
availability for our top 200 SKUs through better vendor coordination and store-level inventory 
tracking could generate $1.2M in recovered sales annually. 
3. Shrinkage Rate:  
Shrinkage (inventory loss from theft/damage) directly erodes margins. To reduce the 
shrinkage rate, our loss prevention roadmap includes RFID tagging for high-theft categories and 
employee awareness training to target 1.6% within 18 months. 
New Product Launch Speed:  
This tracks days from product selection to shelf placement. Streamlining approval 
processes and vendor partnerships can reduce this to 30 days can help us capitalize on trends, 
enabling Ragland to better compete with fast-fashion chains. 
Learning & Growth Perspective 
1. Employee Training Hours:  
Annual training hours per employee build skills in customer engagement and new tech. 
We recommend implementing monthly "service excellence" workshops. Each additional 10 
training hours correlates with 3% higher CSAT scores.  
2. Employee Retention Rate:  
High retention (%) preserves institutional knowledge of local customers. Improving the 
rate, through better scheduling and career path programs can save $200K annually in 
recruitment/training while preserving institutional knowledge.

3. Tech Adoption Rate:  
This measures how quickly staff utilize tools like mobile checkout devices. Only 40% of 
staff use existing tools effectively. Our change management plan includes "tech champions" on 
each shift to drive adoption to 75% within 6 months, critical for inventory accuracy. 
4. Employee Suggestions Implemented:  
Counting staff ideas used quarterly fosters innovation. Implementing just 2 more 
suggestions per quarter (from current 5 to 7) can drive $50K-$100K in annual operational 
savings while boosting engagement. 
NEXT STEPS 
One year after the implementation of Balanced Scorecard, Ragland’s management team 
needs to review the overall progress of the set objectives. The mixed results in these objectives 
are both expected and valuable for us. There are chances of shortcomings in the planning or 
execution that might have been missed earlier which then led to failure for some of the 
objectives. The target for us now would be to identify and assess such issues, analyze any 
interdependencies among other things and find a solution for them. Balanced Scorecard is an 
iterative tool, so such shortcomings are expected, the idea remains to focus on them and modify 
strategies to target these objectives in the forthcoming year. Making these changes to strategy, 
allocating budget to these initiatives would help us reach all its objectives over the years and 
Ragland can build a resilient path to long-term success. Ongoing commitment to the BSC will 
ensure that performance improves not just in isolated metrics, but across the entire organization.
